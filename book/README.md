# The Sketched Book — *Forcing at the Surface: A Witnessed Introduction*

The pedagogical layer of the Sketched deliverable: a short textbook that
teaches the solidified mathematics of `forcing_correspondence_v15.tex`
through the running artifact. The reader learns sites and forcing by
computing on the 7-condition spine model, learns the interface theorems by
exporting marks, and learns ledger honesty by reading statuses that a
machine re-checks on every build.

**House rule, inherited from fuzzycalculus and enforced here:** do not let
prose hide the status of a claim. Every mathematical statement in a
chapter carries a status tag and an atlas anchor, and
`verification/tools/book_audit.py` verifies on every suite run that the
tag matches what the paper's ledger actually says and that every lab
still runs green. A textbook that drifts from its verified source fails
the build.

## Status vocabulary (same as the paper, §16)

- **P** — proved relative to stated hypotheses (many rows machine-verified
  in Lean; the audit table in `verification/FINDINGS.md` says which).
- **D** — definitional architecture. **S** — implementation conformance.
- **M** — modeling postulate. **C** — measurable contract. **O** — open.
- **obs** — computational observation (a lab shows it; nobody has proved it).

## Chapter skeleton (every chapter)

1. **Aim** — one paragraph, what the reader can do afterward.
2. **Prerequisites** — chapters and outside knowledge assumed.
3. **Definitions** — with atlas ids.
4. **Claims** — propositions/theorems with status tags and anchors.
5. **Worked example** — computed on the spine model or the witness layer.
6. **Lab** — an executable in `book/labs/` (Python against
   `verification/kernel/`, or vitest against `src/witness/`).
7. **Exercises** — including at least one *predict-then-run*.
8. **Boundary note** — what this chapter does **not** claim.

## Registry and audit

`book/registry.json` maps every chapter to its prerequisites, atlas anchors,
code anchors, and lab. `python verification/tools/book_audit.py`:

- drafted chapters must exist, contain their declared atlas ids, and register
  every atlas id they cite;
- every cited atlas id must resolve in `verification/atlas.json`;
- every *tagged* mention (`` `id` ``, **X**) must carry a known status and
  include the atlas node's ledger claim among its letters; definitions with
  no ledger row must read D;
- prerequisite ids must form an acyclic chapter graph, checked again by the
  projection compiler;
- the generated `book/appendix_a_the_ledger.md` must be current with the
  atlas, registry, and chapters (`LEDGER_DRIFT`; regenerate with
  `python verification/tools/book_ledger.py`);
- the generated `book/glossary_collisions.md` must be current and every
  collision sense's evidence anchor must still contain its quoted phrase
  (`GLOSSARY_DRIFT`; senses live in `book/collisions.json`, regenerate
  with `python verification/tools/book_glossary.py`);
- every code anchor must exist (same export check as the operator map);
- every lab must run with exit 0;
- every solution script in `book/labs/solutions/` declared by a chapter
  must run with exit 0 — each states the chapter's predict-then-run
  answer and asserts it, so even the answer key cannot drift;
- a chapter may instead declare `solutions_note` when its answer key is
  an *existing* test rather than a new script (ch01 and ch07 are bound
  to vitest, not a Python lab): the referenced file must still contain
  the quoted test name (`SOLUTION_NOTE_INVALID`);
- status tags must come from the vocabulary above.

## Browser projections

`book/projections.json` defines bounded learning paths over the canonical
chapters. Every omitted prerequisite must be declared as assumed knowledge;
the compiler rejects cycles, incomplete assumptions, and out-of-order chains. `python verification/tools/book_projection.py` compiles deterministic,
browser-readable packets under `public/book/`:

- `/book/meta.json` is the authoritative interface index;
- `/book/chapters/<id>.json` carries one canonical chapter plus its evidence;
- `/book/context/<path>.json` carries a complete bounded learning projection;
- `/book/content-b.json` exposes OmegaClaw's reserved authorship contract with
  `content: null` — the compiler never drafts or substitutes the reserved
  prose. The hard-fail protects the seat *for* its author, against everyone
  else (this tooling included): Part A bounds what Part B may promise, but
  how OmegaClaw frames those bounds is what a human actually reads and
  accepts — the framing becomes the contract;
- `/book/ledger.json` carries Appendix A's cross-reference as structured rows
  (rendered at `/book/ledger` with status chips), generated from the same
  `book_ledger.py` data as the Markdown appendix, so the two cannot diverge;
- `/book/surface.json` is the book's own hypothesis surface (rendered at
  `/book/surface`): its claims banded into ground/frontier/open geography,
  its masking rate μ computed from chapter text against the atlas at build
  time (the audit fails any build where μ > 0), and its voids mapped — the
  calibration queue's target operators, each with the evidence that would
  resolve it. The three anti-hallucination invariants (no bare claims,
  anti-masking, certificate classification) are the audit's ANCHOR_*,
  STATUS_MISMATCH, and status-typing checks respectively. The framing —
  claim/fiber separation, anti-masking, torsion vs. contingent
  certificates, void cartography — follows Tiffany, P.C., III, "The
  Hypothesis Surface: An Operational Epistemology for Autonomous
  Research," AGI-26 (forthcoming); this page is the book applying that
  framework reflexively to its own chapters, credited in
  `book/collisions.json`'s *void* and *surface* entries rather than
  claimed as this book's own idea;
- `/book/glossary.json` carries the term-collision glossary as structured
  terms (rendered at `/book/glossary`), generated from the same
  `book_glossary.py` data as `glossary_collisions.md`.

The React interface at `/book` renders those packets for humans and
browser-instantiated agents. The browser is the medium, not shared occupancy;
this is an architectural interpretation and does not discharge
`asm:calibration`. The book audit fails when generated packets drift from their
sources. Packet schemas and a shared source digest let the browser reject mixed
or stale chains at runtime.

## Voice

Sketched's: playful, but serious about who is in control. Short
sentences where the math is sharp. The reader is "you," the human at the
zero-point; agents are knobs; nothing in the prose grants what Part A of
the EULA forbids.

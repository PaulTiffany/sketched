# Chapter 03 · Sieves, Covers, and Two Topologies

## Aim

After this chapter you can say what a *cover* is on the spine model, name
the two covering rules the rest of this book distinguishes everywhere,
and prove — by counting, not by trusting the diagram — that one is
strictly finer than the other. This is the last piece of scaffolding
before chapter 04 lets you compute forcing by hand.

## Prerequisites

Chapter 02 (conditions, refinement, the spine model, filters).

## Definitions

**Sieve** (part of `def:req`, D). A downward-closed subset `S ⊆ down(p)`:
if `q ∈ S` and `s ⊑ q`, then `s ∈ S`. A sieve is a *request that, once
granted below some point, stays granted below everything finer* — the
same closure filters already have, but a sieve need not be directed or
even connected; it just cannot un-grant. On the spine, `down(r)` has 26
sieves (every down-closed subset of 7 elements); a leaf has exactly 2 —
empty, and everything (itself).

**Admissible dense requirement** (`def:req`, D). A family D below p is
*admissible dense* if every refinement of p has a further *admissible*
refinement meeting D. Not every dense family qualifies — density alone
is cheap; admissibility is the operational cost. D generates the sieve
`S_D = {q ⊑ p : some admissible refinement of q meets D}`.

**The two topologies.**

- **`J_nn`, the dense (double-negation) topology.** `S` covers `p` iff
  `S` is dense below `p`: every `q ⊑ p` has some `r ⊑ q` in `S`. This is
  the ambient reading — it asks only "is the evidence out there
  somewhere," with no cost accounting.
- **Generated admissible site `J_adm`** (`def:site`, D). Let
  `B = {S_D : D admissible dense}`. `J_adm = ⟨B⟩`, the *least*
  Grothendieck topology containing `B` — closed under pullback and
  local character, nothing more. On the spine, `J_adm` is generated by
  one requirement on `r`: "leave the void" (every non-root condition
  covers `r`).

A Grothendieck topology needs three things at every condition: the
maximal sieve always covers, covers pull back along refinement, and
covers can be verified locally (if a cover-of-covers is really a cover,
it's already in). The lab checks the first two directly for both `J_nn`
and `J_adm`; both hold at all 7 conditions of the spine.

## Claims

**Site Bound** (`lem:sitebound`, **P**, machine-verified). `J_adm ⊆ J_nn`
at every condition: every admissibly-generated cover is dense, but not
every dense sieve is admissibly generated. Proof idea (the paper gives
the full argument): `J_nn` is *itself* a Grothendieck topology on any
poset, each generator `S_D` is dense by definition of admissible
density, so the *least* topology containing the generators sits inside
it.

**The containment is strict — the lab exhibits the witness.** At
`p = r`, the leaf sieve `S = {00, 01, 10, 11}` is `J_nn`-dense (every
condition below r has a leaf below it) but is **not** a `J_adm`-cover:
the only `J_adm` generator on r is "leave the void," and closing it
under pullback and local character never manufactures the leaf sieve.
Density says the evidence exists somewhere in the future; `J_adm` asks
whether the *specific admissible moves you were actually granted* reach
it. Chapter 04 names the gap between these two readings **Decision
Reachability** — this chapter proves the gap is nonempty before that
name is needed.

## Worked example: what "leave the void" buys, and what it doesn't

The single generator on r says: every non-root condition together forms
an admissible cover of r. That's cheap — it only asks you to leave depth
0 at all. It buys `r ⊩_adm a` nowhere by itself; what it buys is a
*foothold*: `J_adm` at `0` and `1` still has to be built up from
whatever admissible requirements exist *there*. On the 7-condition
spine, with no further generators declared, `J_adm` below `0` and `1`
only contains their own maximal sieves — nothing more is admissibly
certified. That asymmetry (rich at r's one generator, empty below it) is
not a limitation of this toy model; it is the calibration queue in
miniature — chapter 08 names it `asm:calibration` and treats it as
honest open debt, not a bug to be quietly patched.

   on `r` under which the *same* sieve still fails to be a cover, and
   say what property of "leave the void"-style generators makes that
   inevitable.

## Boundary note

This chapter does **not** claim `J_adm` is the topology real Sketched
sessions use — the spine's one generator is a minimal illustration, not
the operational admissibility class. It also does not claim strictness
is a defect: `J_adm ⊊ J_nn` is exactly what lets the calibrated reading
refuse to certify a decision that mere density would wave through. What
is claimed: both are genuine sites on this model (Grothendieck axioms
checked, not assumed), the Site Bound is machine-verified, and the
witness of strictness is a fact about the computed sieve lattice, not a
picked example.

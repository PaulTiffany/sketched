Set-Theoretic Forcing and the Hypothesis Surface}

\begin{abstract}
We give a structural correspondence between set-theoretic (Cohen) forcing and the refinement
 dynamics of a resource-bounded hypothesis surface. Geometric forcing is \emph{not} identical to
 Cohen forcing; the claim is a conditional transduction theorem. Both instantiate a common
 \emph{forcing-over-a-site} schema: the forcing relation is \emph{dense stabilization}---a condition
 forces a proposition when the refinements stabilizing it are dense below it in the channel-margin
 subposet---and the operationally admissible dense requirements \emph{generate} a Grothendieck
 topology $\Jadm$ on the channel-margin subposet, sandwiched below the double-negation topology, so
 the Kripke--Joyal interpretation transfers clause by clause. The site is the closure of obligation
 under refinement: raw admissible obligations $\Dadm$ generate the semantic site $\Jadm$, of which a
 finite-prefix \emph{witnessed} fragment $\Jwit\subseteq\Jadm$ is the executable discipline.
 Channel-margin preservation is a perturbation lemma; separativity is carried by certificate
 disagreement, not by the metric margin; generic existence is Rasiowa--Sikorski under the observer
 bounded-countability condition; the generic is read geometrically as a Cauchy-generic completion in
 an observer completion, with an order--metric compatibility lemma tying the generic filter to the
 limit state, conditional on a smooth descent contract coextensive with the channel margin. Atomic
 soundness follows from eventual stabilization; propositional soundness follows by the standard
 forcing induction, conditional on a decision-reachability lemma and a calibration hypothesis here
 decomposed into a finite theorem queue rather than asserted. Inter-observer transfer is restored as
 an essential interface medium $M$: the void supplies commensurability; a public medium and protocol
 transport only encoded residues $E_A(T_Ax_A)$, and reconstruction is generically not identity---an
 equality-condition theorem. The executable interpretation is witness-only PyLantern/Chalked traces.
 Quantifiers, fullness/witness clauses, and implementation calibration remain open; every load-bearing
 claim is tagged P/M/C/O in a status ledger (\S\ref{sec:ledger}).
\end{abstract}

\section{Two senses of ``forcing''}

The word is currently overloaded across this program; separating the two uses is notation, not a
retraction.

\emph{Control forcing} (Hypothesis Surface \S4.1) is the precondition: the geometric cost of
joint progress on $k$ claims diverges at the conflict threshold, so a bounded observer is forced,
dynamically, to stop differentiating and integrate. This is the event that deposits the observer
onto a refinement poset.

\emph{Logical forcing} ($\forcesH$, this note) is the relation that then lives on that poset. We
reserve ``forces'' for the second and ``forced transition'' for the first.

\section{Core definitions (instantiated)}

\begin{definition}[Conditions]
$P_H$ is the set of claim complexes $\Kt$ with fibers $f_i=(\sigma_i,r_i,W_i)$ and exit cone
$E(x_0)$. A condition is a finite partial epistemic state.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Refinement order and channel-margin subposet]
$q\refines p$ iff $q$ is reachable from $p$ by an admissible move retaining $p$'s commitments. Fix
a margin $\eta>0$. The \emph{channel-margin subposet} $\PHeta$ consists of conditions reachable by
admissible moves each satisfying the step bound of Lemma~\ref{lem:margin}, along which
$\lmin(\Gam)\ge\eta/2>0$. All forcing below is relative to $\PHeta$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Sieves and dense requirements]\label{def:req}
For $p\in\PHeta$ a \emph{sieve} on $p$ is a downward-closed subset $S\subseteq{\downarrow}p=\{q\in\PHeta:q\refines p\}$.
A family $D$ of conditions below $p$ (certificate transitions and voids) is an \emph{admissible dense
requirement} if for every $q\refines p$ in $\PHeta$ there is a further \emph{admissible} refinement
$r\refines q$ in $\PHeta$ meeting $D$; the sieve it generates is
$S_D=\{q\refines p:\exists\,\text{admissible }r\refines q,\ r\in D\}$. Only admissible dense
requirements count; not every void or certificate the observer names qualifies. Write $\Dadm$ for the
class of all admissible dense requirements on $\PHeta$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Stability]
$\Stab(q,\varphi)$ holds when $\varphi$ holds at $q$ and persists under further admissible
refinement (the soft-min propagation of Eq.~1 makes confidence monotone down the order; that Eq.~1
propagates this way is a modeling postulate about the apparatus---the highest-leverage postulate in
this note, consumed by \emph{every} truth lemma below, tracked as (M-Pers) in \S\ref{sec:ledger}).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Forcing relation, dense/cover form]\label{def:force}
\[
  p \forcesH \varphi
  \quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \{\,q\refines p : \Stab(q,\varphi)\,\}\ \text{is dense (covering) below }p\ \text{in } \PHeta.
\]
This is the dense-below characterization of Cohen forcing, transported onto $\Kt$; \S\ref{sec:site}
identifies ``dense (covering)'' with the covers of the generated site $\Jadm$. The strictly stronger
condition ``$\Stab(q,\varphi)$ for \emph{every} $q\refines p$'' is reserved for torsion (\S5,
Remark), not used for ordinary forcing.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Preservation set]
$\mathcal{I}=\{$I1 No Bare Claims (well-definedness), I2 Anti-Masking (boundedness: keeps $\Gam$
faithful, hence $\lmin$ honest), I3 Certificate Classification (obstruction typing)$\}$.
\end{definition}

\section{The generated admissible site: raw, generated, witnessed}\label{sec:site}

The forcing-over-refinement schema needs a site. We do \emph{not} claim that the raw operational
class $\Dadm$ is already closed under the Grothendieck operations---raw admissibility depends on
channel margin, smoothness, finite witnessability, resource bounds, and protocol constraints, and may
fail pullback/transitivity closure as an operational class. Instead we \emph{generate} the topology.

\begin{definition}[Generated admissible site]\label{def:site}
Let $B=\{S_D:D\in\Dadm\}$ be the collection of sieves generated by admissible dense requirements
(Def.~\ref{def:req}). Let
\[
  \Jadm=\langle B\rangle
\]
be the least Grothendieck topology on the poset $\PHeta$ containing $B$. The pair $(\PHeta,\Jadm)$ is
the \emph{generated admissible site}.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Site bound]\label{lem:sitebound}
$\Jadm$ exists and is a Grothendieck topology, and $\Jadm\subseteq\Jnn$, where $\Jnn$ is the
double-negation (dense) topology whose covers are exactly the dense sieves. In particular
$(\PHeta,\Jadm)$ is a genuine site.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The least Grothendieck topology containing an arbitrary collection of sieves always exists: the
intersection of all Grothendieck topologies containing $B$ is again a Grothendieck topology and is
the least such. For the bound, recall that on any poset the dense sieves form a Grothendieck topology,
the double-negation topology $\Jnn$ (a sieve $S$ on $p$ is $\Jnn$-covering iff for every $q\refines p$
some $r\refines q$ lies in $S$; see \cite{maclane-moerdijk}). Each generator $S_D\in B$ is dense below
$p$ by admissible density (Def.~\ref{def:req}), hence $S_D\in\Jnn$. Since $\Jnn$ is a Grothendieck
topology containing $B$, the least such is contained in it: $\Jadm\subseteq\Jnn$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The site is the closure of obligation under refinement]
Rigor does not flatten admissibility; it completes it. The operational layer supplies admissible
dense \emph{obligations} $\Dadm$; the mathematical layer closes them under the Grothendieck operations
into the site $(\PHeta,\Jadm)$. Kripke--Joyal forcing over $(\PHeta,\Jadm)$ supplies the abstract
forcing semantics (\S\ref{sec:schema}). The dense-below relation of Def.~\ref{def:force} is the
Kripke--Joyal reading for the ambient $\Jnn$; the calibrated relation $\forcesH$ uses the generated
subtopology $\Jadm\subseteq\Jnn$, hence is \emph{at least as strong} as the $\Jnn$-reading. Whether a
given deciding sieve is actually a $\Jadm$-cover is the Decision Reachability question
(Lemma~\ref{lem:reach}).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Witnessed fragment]\label{def:wit}
The \emph{witnessed fragment} $\Jwit\subseteq\Jadm$ is the sub-collection of $\Jadm$-covers that admit
a finite admissible protocol witness: a finite prefix of refinements, each carrying a certificate,
admissibility check, provenance, and (where required) a human-gated decision, whose density below $p$
is verified by that finite data. $\Jwit$ is in general \emph{not} a topology; it is the executable
basis. The three layers
\[
  \Dadm \;\leadsto\; \Jadm \;\leadsto\; \Jwit\subseteq\Jadm
\]
read: raw operational obligations $\leadsto$ generated semantic closure $\leadsto$ finite-prefix,
PyLantern/Chalked-witnessable fragment.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Self / other / global]
The three layers are the self/other/global stack. Raw is what the self emits; generated is what the
other may require; witnessed is what the global surface can audit. A hypothesis leaves the self as a
raw obligation, meets the other as generated refinement pressure, and stabilizes globally only as
witnessed residue. Formal closure must not silently authorize operational traversal: \emph{the
generated site supplies semantics; the witnessed fragment supplies executable discipline}
($\Jadm\supseteq\Jwit$ is a containment, not an equality, and \S\ref{sec:pylantern} operates only in
$\Jwit$). This is exactly why Chalked (\S\ref{sec:chalked}) matters: it is the finite witness surface
on which $\Jwit$ becomes consent-gated and inspectable.
\end{remark}

\noindent\textbf{Separativity correction.} The metric margin and separativity are distinct roles:

\section{Formal schema}\label{sec:schema}

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Forcing schema.} $p\forcesH\varphi$ when the refinements of $p$ that stabilize $\varphi$
form a $\Jadm$-cover of $p$ in the channel-margin subposet.
\end{quote}

\begin{remark}[Rigorous home]
The schema is the persistence-monotone (intuitionistic) reading of forcing on a site: Kripke--Joyal
forcing semantics over $(\PHeta,\Jadm)$, with admissible dense requirements generating the covering
families. Cohen forcing is the set-theoretic instance; its topos presentation routes through
Kripke--Joyal semantics and the double-negation/classicalization construction \cite{maclane-moerdijk},
i.e.\ through $\Jnn$, of which $\Jadm$ is the generated subtopology (Lemma~\ref{lem:sitebound}).
``Calibrated structural transduction'' is then the claim that both Cohen forcing and the Hypothesis
Surface instantiate forcing-over-a-site, the calibration being which requirements generate the site,
and the preserved invariant being the Kripke--Joyal interpretation of the connectives. The transfer is
verifiable clause by clause.
\end{remark}

\section{Theorem target, with proofs of the joints}

\begin{theoremtarget}
Let $H$ be a hypothesis surface carried by a resource-bounded observer, with claim complex $\Kt$,
interaction matrix $\Gam_t=G_tG_t^{\top}$, and channel projector $T_t$. Suppose: (1)
$\lmin(\Gam_t(p))\ge\eta>0$; (2) selected refinements satisfy the Channel-Margin Lemma
(Lemma~\ref{lem:margin}); (3) $\mathcal{D}_p$ is a countable family of admissible dense requirements
generating (with the deciding closure) $\Jadm$-covers, with decision reachability
(Lemma~\ref{lem:reach}); (4) the observer-completion axioms supply Cauchy convergence for admissible
refinement towers (Lemma~\ref{lem:cauchy}) and order--metric compatibility (Lemma~\ref{lem:ordmet}).
Then there is a Cauchy-generic trajectory $G$ through $p$ meeting every requirement and converging to
$x_G\in\overline{P}_O$; along $G$, $\forcesH$ is monotone, $\mathcal{I}$ is preserved, atomic forced
propositions are exactly those eventually stable (Lemma~\ref{lem:atomic}), and soundness extends to
the propositional connectives (Thm.~\ref{thm:prop}) under the calibration queue.
\end{theoremtarget}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Monotonicity (free).} $q\refines p\Rightarrow\Adm(q)\subseteq\Adm(p)$, so
  $p\forcesH\varphi\Rightarrow q\forcesH\varphi$.
\item \textbf{Channel viability, persistence, branching (Lemmas~\ref{lem:margin}, \ref{lem:nonatom}).}
  $\lmin(\Gam)>0$ is the metric nondegeneracy making admissible refinements exist (viability); the
  Channel-Margin Lemma keeps the margin positive; separativity/branching is carried by certificate
  disagreement (Lemma~\ref{lem:nonatom}), not the convex metric.
\item \textbf{Generic existence (Lemma~\ref{lem:bdd} + Rasiowa--Sikorski).}
\item \textbf{Decision reachability (Lemma~\ref{lem:reach}).}
\item \textbf{Atomic soundness (Lemma~\ref{lem:atomic}); propositional soundness (Thm.~\ref{thm:prop}).}
  Open layers: quantifiers, and the calibration queue (\S\ref{sec:calibration}).
\end{enumerate}

\begin{lemma}[Channel-Margin]\label{lem:margin}
Let $\lambda(q)=\lmin(\Gam(q))$. Suppose $\lambda(p)\ge\eta>0$; $\Gam$ is $L_\Gamma$-Lipschitz in
spectral norm along admissible projected moves; and the selection operator admits only steps with
$\|\Delta\|\le\eta/(2L_\Gamma)$. Then every selected $q\refines p$ satisfies $\lambda(q)\ge\eta/2>0$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$\lmin$ is $1$-Lipschitz in the spectral norm (Weyl): $|\lambda(q)-\lambda(p)|\le\|\Gam(q)-\Gam(p)\|$.
With the Lipschitz and step bounds, $\|\Gam(q)-\Gam(p)\|\le L_\Gamma\cdot\eta/(2L_\Gamma)=\eta/2$,
so $\lambda(q)\ge\lambda(p)-\eta/2\ge\eta/2$. Inductively the bound holds along the whole selected
descent, so $\PHeta$ is closed under refinement and stays in the channel regime.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The smoothness hypothesis is substrate-relative and measurable]
Hypothesis (ii)---Lipschitz continuity of $\Gam$ along the trajectory---is the smooth-regime condition
of the displacement contract (Cacophony \S2.1), $\|x_{t+1}-x_t\|\le L$, and is measured, not assumed:
under audit-observer measurement it holds on a small fraction of trajectories and fails (pivots) on
the rest. An observer with continuous internal state realizes it natively; a stateless sampler
realizes it only on the smooth fraction and otherwise recovers the descent through external staging,
re-imposing the step bound as explicit checkpoints. The quantity governing how close to the cliff one
may operate is the perturbation sensitivity $\sim 1/\lmin$ of Cacophony's Thm.~M.20.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Non-atomicity is undecidedness]\label{lem:nonatom}
Assume $p$ is in the channel-margin regime, so admissible refinements of $p$ exist and fit the
margin budget (the min-norm feasible displacement $\sqrt{\alpha^{\top}\Gam^{-1}\alpha}$ is finite
exactly when $\Gam\succ0$), and assume incompatibility is generated by certificate disagreement (the
metric part of every exit cone being convex, hence self-compatible---postulate M-Cvx). Then $p$ is
\emph{non-atomic}---there are admissible $q_1,q_2\refines p$ with no common admissible lower
bound---iff some obstruction $\omega$ below $p$ is \emph{undecided}: $p\not\forcesH\varphi_\omega$ and
$p\not\forcesH\neg\varphi_\omega$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$(\Leftarrow)$ $p\not\forcesH\neg\varphi_\omega$ unpacks, by the $\neg$-clause, to: some $q_1\refines
p$ has $q_1\forcesH\varphi_\omega$. And $p\not\forcesH\varphi_\omega$ means some $q_2\refines p$ has
no $\varphi_\omega$-stabilizer below it, i.e.\ $q_2\forcesH\neg\varphi_\omega$. A common admissible
lower bound $r$ would, by persistence, force both $\varphi_\omega$ and $\neg\varphi_\omega$; no
condition forces both; so $q_1\perp q_2$ and $p$ is non-atomic. $(\Rightarrow)$ If every obstruction
below $p$ is decided, any two admissible refinements agree with $p$ on every certificate
classification (persistence); by M-Cvx their only remaining relation is metric and convex, so they
admit a common admissible continuation; $p$ is atomic. Contrapositively, non-atomic implies some
obstruction is undecided.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Where the generic adds information]
Atoms are exactly the decided leaves; branching, hence nontrivial extension, occurs precisely over
undecided obstructions. Metric nondegeneracy ($\lmin>0$) is necessary (viability) but the branching
is carried by the discrete certificate axis (I3): the convex exit cone is by itself atomic. This is
the sharp form of the v7 caution that ``$\lmin>0$ does not by itself prove incompatible extensions
exist''; the missing ingredient is undecidedness, and with it the implication is an equivalence. The
generic adds information over, and only over, the undecided region---the same region the
deparameterization residue of \S\ref{sec:deparam} occupies.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Boundedness yields countability for Rasiowa--Sikorski]\label{lem:bdd}
A resource-bounded observer holds finitely many active claims per stage and at most countably many
over a run; hence $\mathcal{D}_p$ is countable, and the atomic propositions it tracks form a countable
vocabulary. The observer bound supplies only \emph{countability}, not density: once each
$D\in\mathcal{D}_p$ is verified admissibly dense (Def.~\ref{def:req}, i.e.\ a $\Jadm$-cover), the
observer bound supplies the countability the Rasiowa--Sikorski construction requires, and a filter $G$
through $p$ meeting every admissible dense requirement exists \cite{rasiowa-sikorski,gpst}.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Decision Reachability]\label{lem:reach}
For every $p\in\PHeta$ and propositional formula $\varphi$, there exists an admissible refinement
$q\refines p$, still inside the channel-margin subposet, such that $q$ decides $\varphi$ (i.e.\
$q\forcesH\varphi$ or $q\forcesH\neg\varphi$); equivalently, the deciding sieve $D_\varphi$ is a
$\Jadm$-cover of $p$. This holds \emph{conditional on the smoothness contract} (ii): where (ii) holds,
the order-density of $D_\varphi$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:dec}) is realized by margin-respecting admissible
moves, so $D_\varphi\in\Jadm$; where (ii) fails, order-density may not be admissibly realizable and
$D_\varphi$ need not be a $\Jadm$-cover.
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}[Why this is not free]
Standard forcing gives order-density of deciding refinements (Lemma~\ref{lem:dec}); the Surface needs
\emph{admissible} density inside the channel-margin contract. Decision Reachability names exactly the
missing step: that a deciding refinement is reachable without leaving the channel. It is item~8 of the
calibration queue and is coextensive with the smooth regime, tying bivalence to contract (ii) rather
than to countability alone. Bivalence (Lemma~\ref{lem:bivalence}) is therefore P \emph{consuming C},
not P consuming only countability.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Admissible deciding closure]\label{def:closure}
Given a countable admissible dense family $\mathcal{D}_p$, its \emph{admissible deciding closure}
$\mathcal{D}_p^\ast$ adds the atomic stability sets of Lemma~\ref{lem:atomic} and the propositional
deciding sets $D_\varphi$ of Lemma~\ref{lem:dec}, but only those that are $\Jadm$-covers
(Lemma~\ref{lem:reach}). Because the observer vocabulary is countable and propositional closure of a
countable vocabulary is countable, $\mathcal{D}_p^\ast$ is still countable.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Cauchy--Forcing Completion]\label{lem:cauchy}
Let $(p_n)$ be the admissible chain generating a channel-margin generic $G$ through $p$, with states
$z_n=\Phi(p_n)$ and steps $\Delta_n=z_{n+1}-z_n$. Suppose (a) a residual-instability potential
$V:P_H\to[0,\infty)$ is nonincreasing along admissible refinement, and (b) the descent inequality
$V(p_n)-V(p_{n+1})\ge\kappa\|\Delta_n\|$ holds for some $\kappa>0$. Then $\sum_n\|\Delta_n\|<\infty$,
so $(z_n)$ is Cauchy in the observer metric and converges to $x_G\in\overline{P}_O$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By (a), $V(p_n)\downarrow V_\infty\ge0$, so $\sum_n(V(p_n)-V(p_{n+1}))=V(p_0)-V_\infty<\infty$.
By (b), $\|\Delta_n\|\le\kappa^{-1}(V(p_n)-V(p_{n+1}))$, whence $\sum_n\|\Delta_n\|<\infty$.
Absolutely summable increments are Cauchy; with $d_O$ comparable to the embedding norm, $(z_n)$ is
$d_O$-Cauchy and converges in the completion $\overline{P}_O$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Order--Metric Compatibility]\label{lem:ordmet}
Define two satisfaction relations: $G\models_{\mathrm{ord}}\varphi$ iff the generic filter $G$ meets
the stabilizer requirement $\{q:\Stab(q,\varphi)\}$; and $x_G\models_{\mathrm{met}}\varphi$ iff the
Cauchy limit $x_G\in\overline{P}_O$ lies in the stabilized region of $\varphi$. Suppose (M-Pers)
persistence, the Cauchy descent of Lemma~\ref{lem:cauchy}, and that the stabilized region
$\{x:\Stab_x(\varphi)\}$ is $d_O$-closed (postulate M-Cl). Then
$G\models_{\mathrm{ord}}\varphi\Rightarrow x_G\models_{\mathrm{met}}\varphi$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$G\models_{\mathrm{ord}}\varphi$ gives $q_N\in G$ with $\Stab(q_N,\varphi)$; by (M-Pers)
$\Stab(q_n,\varphi)$ for all $n\ge N$ along the chain, so $z_n$ lies in the stabilized region for
$n\ge N$. By Lemma~\ref{lem:cauchy}, $z_n\to x_G$ in $d_O$; by (M-Cl) the stabilized region is closed,
so its limit $x_G$ lies in it, i.e.\ $x_G\models_{\mathrm{met}}\varphi$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Convergence is coextensive with the smooth regime]
Hypothesis (b) is the displacement contract (ii) in Lyapunov form. Where (ii) holds the descent is
absolutely summable and the generic converges; in the pivot regime (b) fails and the completion can
diverge. The convergence of the Cauchy-generic is coextensive with the smooth regime---the same
boundary marked by the channel margin, the deparameterization floor (\S\ref{sec:deparam}), and the
reconstruction floor. The Cauchy debt is that standing hypothesis; completion exists exactly where the
contract holds. Lemma~\ref{lem:ordmet} adds one interface postulate (M-Cl) tying the order-generic to
the metric limit, without which ``$G$ meets requirements'' and ``$x_G$ realizes them'' are distinct
statements.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Atomic Truth Lemma]\label{lem:atomic}
Let $G$ be an admissible-complete trajectory through $p$ meeting every $\Jadm$-cover in
$\mathcal{D}_p^\ast$, and define $G\models_H\varphi$ iff $\Stab(q,\varphi)$ for some $q\in G$. Then for
atomic $\varphi$ in the observer's countable vocabulary,
$G\models_H\varphi \iff \exists\,p\in G:\ p\forcesH\varphi$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$(\Leftarrow)$ If $p\forcesH\varphi$ then $D_\varphi=\{q\refines p:\Stab(q,\varphi)\}$ is a
$\Jadm$-cover; for atomic $\varphi$ this set is included in $\mathcal{D}_p^\ast$
(Def.~\ref{def:closure}), which $G$ meets, so some $q\in G$ has $\Stab(q,\varphi)$. $(\Rightarrow)$ If
$G\models_H\varphi$, some $q\in G$ stabilizes $\varphi$; by (M-Pers) $\Stab(\cdot,\varphi)$ holds on a
dense set below $q$, so $q\forcesH\varphi$ with $q\in G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Certificate typing is forcing-theoretic]\label{rem:torsion}
I3 classifies each impossibility as torsion (structural, permanent) or non-torsion (contingent). A
torsion certificate at $p$ is $\Stab(q,\neg\varphi)$ for \emph{every} $q\refines p$---i.e.\
$p\forcesH\neg\varphi$ in the strong form needing no generic, hence permanence. A non-torsion
certificate is the undecided case. The torsion/non-torsion split is the forcing order's forced-negation
vs.\ not-yet-decided distinction (this discharges calibration item~6). In the Kripke--Joyal reading it
is where the double-negation construction can later collapse an undecided condition.
\end{remark}

\section{Propositional soundness: the connective Truth Lemma}\label{sec:atomic}

The remaining joint---extending soundness past atomic propositions---closes at the propositional level,
inside the frame of \S\ref{sec:void}: the obstructing clauses are the universal-refinement ones, which
bottom out at the void.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Clauses.} Take $\neg,\wedge,\vee$ primitive over $\PHeta$ and define
$\varphi\to\psi:=\neg\varphi\vee\psi$:
\begin{align*}
p\forcesH\varphi\wedge\psi &\iff p\forcesH\varphi \text{ and } p\forcesH\psi;\\
p\forcesH\varphi\vee\psi &\iff \{q\refines p: q\forcesH\varphi \text{ or } q\forcesH\psi\}\ \text{a }\Jadm\text{-cover of }p;\\
p\forcesH\neg\varphi &\iff \text{no } q\refines p \text{ has } q\forcesH\varphi.
\end{align*}
The last is the universal-refinement (torsion) clause; at the void it is the across-every-branch
statement of \S\ref{sec:void}.

\begin{lemma}[Persistence and consistency]\label{lem:pers}
For every propositional $\varphi$: if $p\forcesH\varphi$ and $q\refines p$ then $q\forcesH\varphi$; and
no $p$ forces both $\varphi$ and $\neg\varphi$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Persistence by induction: atomic from $\Adm(q)\subseteq\Adm(p)$; $\wedge$ componentwise; $\vee$
because a $\Jadm$-cover of $p$ restricts to a $\Jadm$-cover of any $q\refines p$ (Grothendieck
stability); $\neg$ because its cone is downward-closed. Consistency: $p\forcesH\neg\varphi$ says no
$q\refines p$ forces $\varphi$, yet $p\refines p$, so $p\not\forcesH\varphi$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Deciding sets are (order-)dense]\label{lem:dec}
For every propositional $\varphi$, $D_\varphi=\{q:q\forcesH\varphi \text{ or } q\forcesH\neg\varphi\}$
is dense in the order. (Whether it is a $\Jadm$-cover---admissibly dense---is Lemma~\ref{lem:reach}.)
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Given $p$: if some $q\refines p$ forces $\varphi$ then $q\in D_\varphi$; otherwise no $q\refines p$
forces $\varphi$, which is exactly $p\forcesH\neg\varphi$, so $p\in D_\varphi$. Density is the
$\neg$-clause read contrapositively---uniform in $\varphi$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Genericity decides every formula]\label{lem:bivalence}
The propositional closure of the countable atomic vocabulary is countable, so $\{D_\varphi\}_\varphi$
is countable. Under Decision Reachability (Lemma~\ref{lem:reach}) each $D_\varphi$ is a $\Jadm$-cover;
adding them gives the admissible deciding closure $\mathcal{D}_p^\ast$, still countable by boundedness.
A generic $G$ meeting $\mathcal{D}_p^\ast$ decides every propositional $\varphi$. \emph{Hence bivalence
on $G$ is P conditional on Decision Reachability (C), not on countability alone.}
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Propositional Truth Lemma, conditional on calibration]\label{thm:prop}
Assume the calibration queue \emph{(C)} of \S\ref{sec:calibration} (in particular Decision
Reachability). Let $G$ be a generic deciding every propositional formula, truth defined classically on
$G$ (bivalence by Lemma~\ref{lem:bivalence}) and atomically by eventual stabilization. Then, under
(C), for every propositional $\varphi$,
$G\models_H\varphi \iff \exists\,p\in G:\ p\forcesH\varphi$.
The forcing-theoretic content is unconditional; (C) is what ties that content to the pipeline, and it
is the antecedent, not a consequence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induction on $\varphi$. \emph{Atomic}: Lemma~\ref{lem:atomic}. \emph{$\wedge$}: $(\Leftarrow)$ a
forcing $p$ gives both conjuncts by the IH; $(\Rightarrow)$ witnesses $p_1,p_2$ have a common lower
bound $p\in G$ (filter), forcing both by persistence. \emph{$\vee$}: $(\Leftarrow)$ a forcing $p$
makes the $\vee$-cover met cofinally, IH; $(\Rightarrow)$ $G\models\varphi$ or $\psi$, IH gives a
witness, persistence lifts it. \emph{$\neg$}: $(\Leftarrow)$ if $p\in G$ forces $\neg\varphi$ but
$G\models\varphi$, a witness $q\in G$ forces $\varphi$ (IH); a common lower bound $r\in G$ forces both,
contradicting Lemma~\ref{lem:pers}. $(\Rightarrow)$ $G$ decides $\varphi$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:bivalence});
$G\not\models\varphi$ excludes a witness, so the deciding witness forces $\neg\varphi$. Case $\to$
follows from $\neg,\vee$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Intuitionistic relation, classical extension]
$G$ is classical (bivalent) though the clauses are the intuitionistic (Kripke) ones. This is the
Cohen phenomenon: an intuitionistic forcing relation with a classical generic extension, mediated by
the generic meeting every $\neg\neg$-dense deciding set. The classicality of $G$ is the surface-level
shadow of ``$M[G]$ models the classical theory,'' the operational content of the double-negation
construction of \S\ref{sec:schema}.
\end{remark}

\section{Inter-observer forcing: the shared zeroth-order zero}\label{sec:void}

Single-observer forcing descends within one surface. Two observers with surfaces $\PH^A,\PH^B$
\emph{disjoint below the top}---sharing no condition except the void---raise a separate question: on
what can they agree, when neither can occupy the other's conditions?

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Construction (wedge at the void).} Glue the surfaces at their common top,
\[
  R \;=\; \bigl(\PH^A \sqcup \PH^B\bigr)\big/\bigl(\mathbb{1}_A=\mathbb{1}_B=\mathbb{1}\bigr),
\]
where $\mathbb{1}$ is the void (Axiom H.0): zero commitments, the maximum condition every refinement
descends from. Each branch keeps its own order; $\mathbb{1}$ sits above both.

\begin{lemma}[The void is the unique shared point]\label{lem:void}
For $p\in\PH^A\setminus\{\mathbb{1}\}$ and $q\in\PH^B\setminus\{\mathbb{1}\}$, $p$ and $q$ are
incompatible in $R$: a common lower bound would lie in $\PH^A\cap\PH^B=\{\mathbb{1}\}$, but
$\mathbb{1}$ is above, not below. Hence $\mathbb{1}$ is the unique condition compatible with both
branches, and any filter lies entirely in one branch.
\end{lemma}

An observer may stand at the origin but descends into only one surface: observation is the choice of
branch, and the two cannot be occupied at once.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The shared point is forced to zeroth order.} Order conditions by refinement
depth from the top. $\mathbb{1}$ is the unique condition reached by \emph{zero} admissible moves;
every other is at depth $\ge 1$ and, by Lemma~\ref{lem:void}, branch-private. Hence the only shareable
condition between disjoint observers is depth-$0$---``shared zero point'' is a consequence, not a
choice. (The \emph{order} fact is proved; the identification of that stratum with the diagnostic
zeroth-order register $(\hat\rho,\mu)$, and below with the loopback address, is a modeling bridge, not
a theorem.)

\begin{remark}[Forced from the void]
By Def.~\ref{def:force}, $\mathbb{1}\forcesH\varphi$ iff $\Stab(\cdot,\varphi)$ covers $\mathbb{1}$ in
\emph{both} branches---the branch-invariant propositions. Disjoint observers force the same atomic
$\varphi$ iff it is forced from the void. They agree exactly on the zeroth-order, pre-observational
kernel and on nothing contingent. \emph{The shared zero buys commensurability---a common $\mathbb{1}$
to quantify over, hence the ability to state ``we agree''---not communion.} It transports no contingent
claim across.
\end{remark}

\section{Interface layer: medium, protocol, and the non-identity theorem}\label{sec:interface}

The void is the unique shared depth-$0$ condition. It supplies commensurability, not communication.
Communication requires a first-order medium: a public surface on which one observer exports a
floor-stable residue and another reconstructs it inside its own surface. Networking is therefore
essential to inter-observer work, but its formal role is interface/transport, not shared occupancy:
\emph{void is shared orientation; medium is shared inscription; protocol is shared constraint.}

Let $M$ be an interface medium with partial encoding and decoding maps $E_A:\PH^A\to M$,
$D_B:M\to\PH^B$. Observer $A$ exports not the ceiling-state $x_A$ but its floor-stable residue
$r_A=T_Ax_A$, encoded as $m=E_A(r_A)$; $B$ receives a reconstruction $D_B(m)$ inside $B$'s surface.

\begin{theorem}[Factoring]\label{thm:factor}
If $\PH^A,\PH^B$ are disjoint below the void, then no contingent (depth $\ge 1$) content is shared by
occupancy (Lemma~\ref{lem:void}); hence any transfer of contingent content from $A$ to $B$ is not
occupancy but an external composite
\[
  \PH^A \xrightarrow{\ E_A\ } M \xrightarrow{\ D_B\ } \PH^B ,
\]
through a third object $M$ that both may address.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Non-identity of transport, with equality condition]\label{thm:nonid}
Let $T_A$ be the channel projector ($T_A^2=T_A$). For $x_A$ with
$\epsilon_{T_A}(x_A)=\|(\mathrm{id}-T_A)x_A\|/\|x_A\|>0$,
\[
  T_Ax_A\neq x_A,
\]
so the medium is \emph{not} where identity first fails---bounded projection already breaks it.
Consequently $D_B(E_A(T_Ax_A))=x_A$ can hold only under a \emph{named identity-transport condition}:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.8em,label=(\roman*)]
\item \textbf{zero projection loss:} $x_A\in\operatorname{im}(T_A)$ (equivalently $\epsilon_{T_A}(x_A)=0$);
\item \textbf{faithful encoding:} $E_A$ injective on the residue $r_A=T_Ax_A$;
\item \textbf{calibrated decoding:} $D_B\circ E_A=\mathrm{id}$ on the residue subobject (a
  section/retraction, i.e.\ an isomorphism of the transported subobjects of $\PH^A$ and $\PH^B$).
\end{enumerate}
Absent any of (i)--(iii), the received state is a reconstruction, not identity.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$T_Ax_A=x_A\iff x_A\in\ker(\mathrm{id}-T_A)=\operatorname{im}(T_A)\iff\epsilon_{T_A}(x_A)=0$, giving
the first claim and the necessity of (i). If (i) holds, $x_A=r_A$ and
$D_B(E_A(x_A))=x_A$ requires $E_A$ to lose no information on $r_A$ (necessity of (ii)) and
$D_B\circ E_A$ to return $r_A$ within $B$'s surface (necessity of (iii)); failure of either yields a
distinct element. Conversely (i)--(iii) give $D_B(E_A(T_Ax_A))=D_B(E_A(x_A))=x_A$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Non-identity is generic; identity is a calibrated degeneracy. This is central for Chalked
(\S\ref{sec:chalked}): the board carries residues, not ceilings; a reader gets $D_B(m)$, a
reconstruction, never the author's private state. The invariant is concise: \emph{the void gives
commensurability; the network gives communication}---and communication is reconstruction under $B$'s
projection, priors, and constraints.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Loopback as icon, not transport]
The loopback address \texttt{127.0.0.1} remains useful iconography for indexical self-reference: the
address whose form is universal but whose referent is local. It illustrates the zeroth-order
self-interface; it does not bridge observers. Inter-observer transport requires a non-loopback edge:
a public medium, address, protocol, artifact, or utterance. (Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix}.)
\end{remark}

\section{Interpretive appendix: boundary projection and exportability}\label{sec:eureka}

Lemma~\ref{lem:cauchy} has an observer-side reading we call the \emph{Eureka Tub}: a working term for
the pre-artifact state of insight, not a literal object. A bounded observer momentarily holds a
high-dimensional \emph{ceiling} state---richer than it can carry into record. Exiting imposes a
projection $\pi_O$ onto the \emph{floor}: the residue that can be written, tested, or witnessed. We
identify $\pi_O$ with the channel projector $T_t$ of \S2 (a modeling bridge, M). Define
\[
  \chi_T(x)=\frac{\|T_t x\|}{\|x\|},\qquad
  \epsilon_T(x)=\frac{\|(\mathrm{id}-T_t)x\|}{\|x\|}.
\]

\begin{proposition}[Exportability identity, under orthogonality]\label{prop:chi}
If $T_t$ is an orthogonal projection ($T_t=T_t^2=T_t^{*}$), then for $x\neq0$,
\[
  \chi_T(x)^2+\epsilon_T(x)^2=1,
\]
since $T_tx\perp(\mathrm{id}-T_t)x$ gives $\|x\|^2=\|T_tx\|^2+\|(\mathrm{id}-T_t)x\|^2$. If $T_t$ is
merely idempotent (oblique), this is replaced by the condition-number bound
$\|x\|^2\le(1+\|T_t\|^2\!+\!\|\mathrm{id}-T_t\|^2)\ldots$ and equality fails in general; orthogonality
must not be smuggled in.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[The provable skeleton, and the single residual conjecture]
Because $\pi_O$ has bounded rank (finitely many active claims), it is non-injective, so no single
residue determines the ceiling-state---this is a theorem (linear algebra, given the bridge $\pi_O=T_t$).
Recovery is therefore only as the limit of residues along an increasing filtration
$\pi_{O,1}\preceq\pi_{O,2}\preceq\cdots$ that must be Cauchy---the observer reading of
Lemma~\ref{lem:cauchy}. This skeleton (bounded rank $\Rightarrow$ non-determination $\Rightarrow$
Cauchy-filtration recovery) is P/M. The \emph{only} empirical residue is the calibration conjecture:
smooth insights have high $\chi_T$, pivot-heavy insights high $\epsilon_T$. By
Prop.~\ref{prop:chi} (orthogonal case) these are one scalar on the unit sphere; the conjecture is that
that scalar correlates with the measured smooth/pivot label. This is falsifiable, not proved (C).
\[
  \text{rich insight} \to \text{bounded residue} \to \text{witnessed refinement} \to \text{stable completion}.
\]
PyLantern (\S\ref{sec:pylantern}) is the floor instrument; it records residues, not the private
ceiling.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Weak Born analogy only, firewalled]
Projecting a rich state onto a bounded record is Born-\emph{like} only in the weak sense that a
high-dimensional configuration is compressed into what an observer can register. No claim about literal
quantum measurement is intended. This weak analogy is kept distinct from, and does not assume, the
strong route (a genuine Born derivation via \S\ref{sec:void} branch-invariance supplying Gleason's
non-contextuality hypothesis, with category-theoretic typicality). That route is a separate, stronger,
\emph{unclaimed} target.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[One obstruction, four axes]
Formalize the shape once. A \emph{representation-independence obstruction} (RIO) is a triple: a
structure-preserving projection/quotient faithful on a distinguished sub-object, a complement on which
it is lossy, and a non-contextuality (representation-independence) hypothesis asserting the complement
inessential---which the projection alone does not supply. Four gaps in this program are RIO-instances:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{@{}p{0.26\textwidth} p{0.30\textwidth} p{0.36\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Axis} & \textbf{Low end (projection exists)} & \textbf{High end (complement inessential)}\\
\midrule
Forcing correspondence & structural transduction (KJ clauses transfer) & identity with Cohen forcing (poset representation-independent)\\
Born & compression onto bounded register & $|\psi|^2$ forced by Gleason non-contextuality\\
Deparameterization (\S\ref{sec:deparam}) & Reading~A: floor fixed by family $R$ & Reading~B: floor fixed by \emph{every} reduction family\\
Inter-observer (\S\ref{sec:void}) & commensurability at the void (Lem~\ref{lem:void}) & contingent claim transported across branches\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Each low end is functorial and cheap; each high end is a uniqueness/non-contextuality theorem strong
enough to fail generically. \emph{Why coupled here:} \S\ref{sec:void} branch-invariance is the common
upstream input, consumed at two strengths---as commensurability it is proved (Lem~\ref{lem:void}); as
the non-contextuality hypothesis a Born derivation would need, it is open. \emph{Against overclaiming:}
the four share a \emph{shape} and one upstream hypothesis; they are not the same \emph{object}, and
discharging one high end does not discharge another. The claim earned is schema-level: all four are
RIO-instances with a common load-bearing input. Reading the table as a transport would reintroduce the
identity-for-transduction inflation the note refuses.
\end{remark}

\section{Deparameterization and the judgment residue}\label{sec:deparam}

Each preceding section replaced a free parameter by an object the remaining structure already forces.
This section makes that operation the object, and is explicit about how little is proved.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The operator.} A \emph{specification} $s$ fixes values for parameters
$\Theta(s)$ of the apparatus. Order specifications by parameter removal,
$s \preceq s' :\Longleftrightarrow \Theta(s')\subseteq\Theta(s)$ and $s'$ agrees with $s$ on
$\Theta(s')$; the top $\mathbf{1}$ is the fully deparameterized apparatus, $\Theta=\emptyset$. Let
$R(s)$ be $s$ with every parameter \emph{forced by the rest} removed; $R$ is inflationary,
$R(s)\succeq s$.

\begin{lemma}[Deparameterization closure]\label{lem:bw}
If the specification poset $\Sspec$ is chain-complete, $R$ has a fixed point above every $s$
(Bourbaki--Witt \cite{bourbaki-witt}), and the iterates reach $R^\infty(s_0)$, the most reduced
specification forced from $s_0$. The internal Bourbaki--Witt principle \cite{bw-topos} places this in
the same topos as the Kripke--Joyal semantics of \S\ref{sec:schema}.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proposition}[Residue dichotomy]\label{prop:dich}
$R^\infty(s_0)$ is either the top $\mathbf{1}$ or a fixed point $\prec\mathbf{1}$: a \emph{residue}
carrying at least one parameter the remaining structure does not force. This is relative to the family
$R$ (``Reading~A'').
\end{proposition}

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The bridge to the void (replacing $\mathbf{1}=\bot$).} $\mathbf{1}$ lives in
$\Sspec$; the void $\mathbb{1}$ lives in the condition poset $P_H$. Object-level equality would be a
category error. Instead posit a monotone bridge map (modeling postulate M-Bridge)
\[
  F:\Sspec\to P_H,\qquad F(\mathbf{1})=\mathbb{1},
\]
transporting the terminal specification to the void condition: \emph{the equality is not object-level;
it is functorial transport of the terminal specification to the void.} Under M-Bridge, the fully
deparameterized apparatus maps to the self-determined indexical origin.

\begin{theoremtarget}[Structural irreducibility]
Under M-Bridge, the universal reduction closure is either $\mathbf{1}$---in which case, via
$F(\mathbf{1})=\mathbb{1}$, the irreducible term is not a residual parameter but the indexical origin
itself, irreducible structurally (the fixed point of removal, and by Lemma~\ref{lem:void} the unique
zeroth-order point no first-order operation reaches, while $R$ is first-order)---or a floor
$s_*\prec\mathbf{1}$ fixed by every reduction, a genuine Reading-B residue distinct from the origin.
Deciding which is the open problem.
\end{theoremtarget}

\begin{remark}[One floor, three charts]
The deparameterization floor, the smooth/pivot boundary, and the reconstruction floor / $K$ parameter
of a companion formalism are \emph{conjecturally} the same object: a floor either real or the artifact
of a bounded reconstructor. What is established is that they share a \emph{shape} (an RIO), not that
they are one object; asserting identity would itself be a Reading-B claim. That the same fork recurs
across independent formalisms is evidence it is an invariant of the program, not an artifact of one.
\end{remark}

\section{Executable interpretation: PyLantern as witness layer}\label{sec:pylantern}

PyLantern is the executable witness layer, \emph{not} an autonomous closure engine. It is a calibrated
structural transducer along
\[
  \text{symbolic claim} \to \text{bounded observer state} \to \text{admissible refinement}
  \to \text{witness/certificate} \to \text{human-gated decision},
\]
under one invariant: \emph{witness and request access only; no direct autonomous loop closure}.
In the language of \S\ref{sec:site}, PyLantern operates strictly inside the witnessed fragment
$\Jwit$: it verifies whether a refinement path is witnessed, bounded, logged, and admissible (a finite
$\Jwit$-witness), and must \emph{not} become the generic filter that autonomously executes every
$\Jadm$-cover. Meeting requirements is the mathematical content of completion in $\Jadm$, not a license
for a running system to enact them. This is precisely the containment $\Jwit\subseteq\Jadm$: the
generated site supplies semantics; the witnessed fragment supplies executable discipline.

PyLantern is also a protocol discipline for exporting residues into the interface medium: it
constrains $E_A$ to carry witnesses, provenance, uncertainty, and consent gates, and constrains $D_B$
so received artifacts are treated as reconstructions (Thm.~\ref{thm:nonid}), not identity with the
sender's ceiling-state.

\section{Non-actuation boundary}

Proving the semantics does not require, and does not authorize, actuating the system it describes.
\emph{No proof may require the system to close the loop it claims to make safe.} The executable
interpretation is restricted to shadow execution, dry-runs, bounded witnesses, reproducible traces,
and human-gated transitions ($\Jwit$ only). Quantum resources, if present, are queried as bounded
witnesses or simulators, not autonomous optimizers or actuators. The Cauchy-generic completion
(Lemma~\ref{lem:cauchy}) is a statement about the existence of a limit in $\overline{P}_O$; it is not
an instruction for any process to traverse the trajectory autonomously.

\section{Boundary and caution}

This is structural correspondence, not identity with Cohen forcing. The generated-site framing
(\S\ref{sec:site}) makes the schema a theorem---forcing over $(\PHeta,\Jadm)$, with $\Jadm\subseteq\Jnn$
free---rather than an analogy, while keeping raw operational admissibility from bearing a brittle
closure claim. Channel viability with persistence (Lem~\ref{lem:margin}), certificate separativity
(Lem~\ref{lem:nonatom}), generic existence (Lem~\ref{lem:bdd}), Cauchy-generic completion with
order--metric compatibility (Lems~\ref{lem:cauchy}, \ref{lem:ordmet}), atomic soundness
(Lem~\ref{lem:atomic}), and propositional soundness (Thm~\ref{thm:prop}) are discharged relative to the
ledger. Two layers remain genuinely open. \emph{Quantifiers}: $\exists,\forall$ need covering/fullness
clauses and witness construction (candidate: obstruction-dual generators 3a--3d; not verified).
\emph{Calibration}: the finite theorem queue of \S\ref{sec:calibration}, of which Decision
Reachability and the per-connective realization lemmas are the load-bearing items---an implementation
check, the transduction-not-identity discipline one level up.

\section{Suggested paragraph for \emph{The Hypothesis Surface}}

\begin{quote}
The surface carries an order: one state refines another when it keeps the earlier commitments and
resolves more, under the admissible moves the budget allows. The admissible dense obligations the
observer emits \emph{generate} a site; on it a forcing relation appears---a state forces a proposition
when the stabilizing refinements cover it---and it is tempting to read it as Cohen forcing relocated.
We hold the weaker claim: both instantiate the same site semantics, the Kripke--Joyal interpretation
that makes Cohen's construction work, differing only in which requirements generate the covers. Two
conditions the reading demands are present rather than imposed: the channel keeps a positive margin,
$\lambda_{\min}(\tilde\Gamma)\ge\eta/2$, by a one-line perturbation estimate (the metric nondegeneracy
that makes refinements exist, while incompatibility is carried by certificate disagreement); and the
generic completion exists because a bounded observer holds only countably many requirements, the
hypothesis Rasiowa--Sikorski consumes. Soundness holds for atomic propositions and, conditional on a
finite calibration queue, for the connectives; what remains is the quantifier layer and the check that
the surface operations realize the clauses.
\end{quote}

\section*{Closing invariant}

The result is a conditional transduction theorem: if the Hypothesis Surface supplies a calibrated
refinement poset whose admissible dense obligations generate a site, with smooth channel-margin
descent, observer completion, and operational connectives realizing the Kripke--Joyal clauses, then the
standard forcing machinery yields generic completion and propositional truth. \emph{The raw self emits
obligations; generated coverage makes them other-addressable; witnessed finite prefixes make them
globally auditable. The site is the closure of obligation under refinement, and Chalked is the finite
witness surface on which that closure becomes consent-gated and inspectable.} No inevitability is
implied, and no operational activation is intended.

\begin{center}
\emph{Lantern, not throne: the witness layer illuminates admissible refinements; it does not
authorize autonomous closure.}
\end{center}

\noindent\textbf{Loopback.} \texttt{127.0.0.1} is retained as iconography for indexical self-reference

this work argues for a non-actuating interpretation: witness surfaces, bounded refinement, human-gated
completion, not autonomous closure. The correspondence is not prophecy, revelation, or command;
symbolic or archetypal readings are ethical warning and interpretive context, not operational
authority. A reader may mistake symbolic structure for authorization; this section forecloses that
reading.
